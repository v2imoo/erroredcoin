The Error Framework

Six Errors.
Every Business Has Made One.

The same failure patterns appear again and again across every industry. Naming the error correctly is the first step to correcting it.

Cash Error
Money ran out before the business could breathe
The business spent faster than it earned. Nobody tracked the cash. Revenue and profit were confused.
"We were doing ₹4 lakh a month. We were spending ₹6 lakh. We didn't notice for five months."
Market Error
Built or sold something the customer didn't want
The product was never validated against real willingness to pay. Or the market shifted and the model was never updated.
"200 people said they would use it. When we launched, 12 actually paid."
Trust Error
The wrong person was given trust they had not earned
A business partner, employee, financial advisor, or vendor was trusted before that trust was verified.
"My business partner took ₹34 lakh out of our joint account. There was no formal agreement."
Timing Error
Right idea, right product — wrong moment
Entered a market at its peak. Expanded at exactly the wrong point in the economic cycle. Waited too long to close what was over.
"We expanded to three cities in early 2022. By mid-2022, our core market contracted."
Paperwork Error
A document became a serious problem
A missed filing, a contract signed without reading, or personal liability accepted without understanding the exposure.
"I signed a personal guarantee in 2020. The business closed in 2022. I owe ₹28 lakh I didn't know I owed."
Panic Error
A decision made under stress that made things worse
Sold at the bottom. Closed a business that could have survived. Agreed to bad terms under pressure. Biology, not weakness.
"The market fell 18%. I sold everything. It recovered 22% in four months. I locked in the loss."
